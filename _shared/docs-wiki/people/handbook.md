# People & Culture Handbook

This handbook covers how we work at Acme Corp — our values, policies, and the practical details of day-to-day employment. It's a living document; changes are proposed via PR and reviewed by People & Legal before merging.

---

## Our values

These aren't posters on the wall — they're the criteria we use when making decisions under uncertainty.

**Ship with confidence** — We move fast but not recklessly. Automated tests, code review, and staged rollouts are how we go fast sustainably.

**Direct, kind communication** — Say what you mean. Disagree openly and respectfully. Silence in the face of a bad decision isn't politeness.

**Own the outcome** — We don't pass the problem to the next person. If you spot something broken, you're responsible for making sure it gets fixed — even if it's not your code.

**Curiosity over certainty** — We operate in a complex domain. Intellectual humility and willingness to be wrong are superpowers here.

## Working hours & flexibility

Acme Corp operates a results-oriented working culture. Core hours — when we expect everyone to be reachable for collaboration — are **10:00–16:00 in your local timezone**.

> [!info]
> We have team members across UK, US East, and India. When scheduling meetings, prefer slots within the 14:00–16:00 UTC window where possible to minimise unsociable hours for any one region.

Outside core hours, you have flexibility on when and where you work. Speak to your manager if your role or team has specific expectations.

## Leave policy

| Leave type | Entitlement | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Annual leave | 25 days + bank holidays | Carry over up to 5 days to January |
| Sick leave | Unlimited (self-certified up to 5 days) | See occupational health policy for long-term illness |
| Parental leave | 26 weeks full pay (primary) / 4 weeks (secondary) | Enhanced beyond statutory minimum |
| Compassionate leave | Up to 5 days | Manager discretion for additional days |
| Study leave | Up to 3 days/year for exams | Requires manager approval 2 weeks in advance |

> [!note]
> Annual leave requests should be submitted in Workday at least 2 weeks in advance for requests of 3+ days. Same-day requests should be discussed with your manager directly.

## Expenses

:::expand Expense policy summary
**Approved without pre-approval (up to £75 per item)**
- Work-related books and online courses
- Conference registration (with manager awareness)
- Home office consumables (pens, notebooks, etc.)

**Require manager pre-approval**
- Equipment purchases (keyboards, monitors, etc.) — up to £500 every 2 years
- Travel and accommodation for company events
- Software subscriptions not on the approved list

**Require VP-level approval**
- Any single expense over £500
- International travel
- Client entertainment

Submit all expenses via Workday within 30 days of purchase. Attach receipts; claims without receipts will be rejected.
:::

> [!warning]
> Personal credit cards and company cards must not be used interchangeably. If you've been issued a company card, use it for business expenses. Personal card claims are fine for approved expenses when a company card isn't practical.

## Performance & development

Performance is discussed in two formal cycles per year (March and September) using the **Impact & Growth** framework. Between cycles, your manager should be running a fortnightly 1:1 — flag it to your skip-level if this isn't happening.

We have a £1,000/year learning budget per person. Spend it on courses, conferences, books, or certifications relevant to your role. Approval required above £500 for a single item.

## Code of conduct

We expect everyone at Acme to:
- Treat colleagues with respect regardless of role, background, or seniority
- Not engage in discrimination, harassment, or bullying of any kind
- Protect confidential company and customer information
- Report concerns to your manager, People team, or via the anonymous ethics hotline

Breaches of the code of conduct are taken seriously and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
